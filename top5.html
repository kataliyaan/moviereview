Top 5 Movies of All Times

We're diving into the world of cinema to bring you the Top 5 Movies of All Time. Whether you're a casual movie-goer or a die-hard film buff, this list is sure to spark some excitement and maybe even a little debate. We've scoured the archives, considered countless reviews, and listened to fan favorites to curate a list of the most iconic, groundbreaking, and unforgettable films ever made.

1

Interstellar

Rating: PASSED
Genre: Sci-fi/Adventure
Release Date: November 6, 2014
Director: Christopher Nolan
Cast: Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Chastain, Bill Irwin, Ellen Burstyn, Michael Caine, and Matt Damon.
Runtime: 169 Minutes
★★★★★

In Earth's future, a global crop blight and second Dust Bowl are slowly rendering the planet uninhabitable. Professor Brand (Michael Caine), a brilliant NASA physicist, is working on plans to save mankind by transporting Earth's population to a new home via a wormhole. But first, Brand must send former NASA pilot Cooper (Matthew McConaughey) and a team of researchers through the wormhole and across the galaxy to find out which of three planets could be mankind's new home.

2

The Godfather

Rating: PASSED
Genre: Crime
Release Date: March 24, 1972
Director: Francis Ford Coppola
Cast: Marlon Brando, Al Pacino, James Caan, Richard Castellano, Robert Duvall.
Runtime: 175 Minutes
★★★★★

Widely regarded as one of the greatest films of all time, this mob drama, based on Mario Puzo's novel of the same name, focuses on the powerful Italian-American crime family of Don Vito Corleone (Marlon Brando). When the don's youngest son, Michael (Al Pacino), reluctantly joins the Mafia, he becomes involved in the inevitable cycle of violence and betrayal. Although Michael tries to maintain a normal relationship with his wife, Kay (Diane Keaton), he is drawn deeper into the family business.
