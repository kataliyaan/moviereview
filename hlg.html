Hello, Love, Goodbye

Synopsis

"Hello, Love, Goodbye" is a 2019 Filipino romantic drama film directed by Cathy Garcia-Molina. The story revolves around Joy Fabregas (Kathryn Bernardo), a domestic helper in Hong Kong who dreams of migrating to Canada for a better life. She works tirelessly to support her family back in the Philippines while navigating the challenges of living and working abroad.

Amidst her struggles, Joy meets Ethan Del Rosario (Alden Richards), a charming bartender and aspiring chef who also works in Hong Kong. Despite their different aspirations and backgrounds, they develop a deep connection and romantic feelings for each other. However, their budding relationship is complicated by their individual dreams and responsibilities, leading them to question whether they can find a way to reconcile their personal ambitions with their growing love for each other.

Plot and Themes

The film explores themes of love, sacrifice, and self-discovery against the backdrop of the overseas Filipino worker (OFW) experience in Hong Kong. It portrays the emotional journey of Joy and Ethan as they navigate the complexities of their careers, relationships, and aspirations, ultimately asking poignant questions about the nature of love and the pursuit of happiness.

Visuals and Cinematography

"Hello, Love, Goodbye" uses vibrant cinematography to portray the bustling cityscape of Hong Kong, contrasting its energetic streets and scenic views with intimate moments that reveal the personal struggles and aspirations of its characters, Joy and Ethan. The film's dynamic camera work and use of lighting capture the essence of urban life while highlighting the emotional journey of the protagonists as they navigate love, sacrifice, and self-discovery in a foreign environment.

Shooting Locations

"Hello, Love, Goodbye" was primarily filmed on location in Hong Kong. Some of the key shooting locations include:

Quarry Bay, Hong Kong: In "Hello, Love, Goodbye," Quarry Bay in Hong Kong is featured as one of the filming locations. Specifically, scenes were shot in and around the Quarry Bay area, known for its mix of residential and commercial spaces, as well as its waterfront views and bustling streets. Quarry Bay provided a backdrop for portraying everyday life in Hong Kong, adding to the authenticity and urban atmosphere depicted in the film.

Quarry Bay, Hong Kong

Central District: Known for its skyscrapers, bustling streets, and vibrant nightlife, Central District provided the backdrop for many urban scenes in the film.

Central District, Hong Kong

Victoria Harbour: The iconic Victoria Harbour, with its stunning views of Hong Kong's skyline and bustling harbor activities, was featured prominently in several scenes.

Victoria Harbour, Hong Kong

Conclusion

"Hello, Love, Goodbye" is a heartfelt story set in Hong Kong, blending vibrant cinematography with themes of love, sacrifice, and personal growth. It follows Joy and Ethan as they navigate the complexities of working abroad, pursuing their dreams, and discovering the resilience of love amidst cultural differences. The film's conclusion resonates with themes of hope and self-discovery, showcasing the transformative power of love and the pursuit of happiness in unfamiliar surroundings.
